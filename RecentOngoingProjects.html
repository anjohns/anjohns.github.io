Recent & Ongoing Projects

Human Movement in Cities

This project explores how the built environment shapes the way pedestrians move through cities. How do urban features attract or repel people? How do we navigate around one another, and what patterns emerge when crowds form? I’m combining agent-based modeling, cellphone mobility data, and machine learning to understand how physical design influences collective behavior at the street level.

Adaptive Cities Group

I’m part of a team developing digital twin models to help cities respond to extreme heat. Our goal is to create a near-real-time loop of data, modeling, and decision-making that can guide targeted cooling interventions—everything from tree planting to infrastructure redesign. The project is focused on protecting vulnerable urban populations from the health and social impacts of climate change. We are piloting this in Santiago, Chile, with seed funding from The Rockefeller Foundation’s partnership with Brookings (17 Rooms) and additional support from AutoDesk.

Zoning and Housing Restrictiveness

Zoning ordinances vary dramatically from one municipality to the next, making it nearly impossible to assemble large-scale datasets on how land is actually zoned. This project aimed to overcome that barrier through modeling and inference. I led a machine learning effort that used features of the built environment to predict the maximum housing density allowed under local zoning codes. By training stacked ensemble models, we were able to estimate zoning restrictiveness at scale—offering a new way to understand and map the regulatory landscape that shapes development.
